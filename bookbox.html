About BookBox

Users design, print and build a small box that looks like their favorite book. In a digital world, BookBox creates a tangible object that prompts re-connection with significant information.

You can read about the original concept by James Bridle here.

How it works

The user enters details for a favorite book - title, author, wordcount, and cover image. Then they can play around with fonts and color to get the design to their liking.

The screen shows a dynamic 3D view of the design, with the height and width of the book scaling based on the wordcount.

Once the user is satisfied with the design, they can print it to a paper net to make their bookbox.

Protyping

In the early stages, prototyping focused on developing functionality, rather than getting bogged down by interface details.

Codepen was handy for prototyping various components.

Bugs

To track the bugs on this project, I used checkbox-style notes in the Google Keep app.
